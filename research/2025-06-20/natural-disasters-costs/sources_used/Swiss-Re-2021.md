# Swiss Re Institute Report: Extreme Flood Events and Insurance Protection Gaps (2021)

## Overview
The Swiss Re Institute's 2021 report provides critical insights into global flood risks, highlighting the massive gap between total economic losses and insured losses. The report emphasizes how climate change and urbanization are contributing to growing catastrophe losses globally.

## Key Global Findings

### Flood Event Summary (2021)
- **Total economic losses**: Significantly high, though exact figure not specified in summary
- **Insured losses**: Considerably lower than total economic losses
- **Major flood events**: Multiple significant flood events across different regions
- **Regional distribution**: Various global regions experienced major flood events

### Insurance Protection Gap
- **75% of flood risks remain uninsured globally**
- This represents a massive protection gap that exacerbates economic impacts
- Particularly acute in developing countries
- Even in developed nations, certain flood risks remain underinsured

## Climate Change Context

### Climate Change as a Driver
Swiss Re explicitly identifies climate change as a key factor contributing to growing catastrophe losses:
- "Climate change and urbanization contributing to growing catastrophe losses"
- Rising global temperatures leading to more extreme precipitation events
- Changing rainfall patterns and increased flood risk
- Sea level rise amplifying coastal flood impacts
- More intense weather events becoming more frequent

### Urbanization Impact
- Rapid urban development in flood-prone areas
- Increased impervious surfaces reducing natural water absorption
- Strain on drainage infrastructure
- Higher concentration of assets in vulnerable areas

## Economic Impact Analysis

### Direct Economic Damages
- Property destruction and damage
- Infrastructure damage (roads, bridges, utilities)
- Agricultural losses
- Business interruption

### Indirect Economic Effects
- Supply chain disruptions
- Economic multiplier effects
- Government response and recovery costs
- Long-term reconstruction costs

### Regional Economic Impact Variations
- Higher absolute losses in developed economies
- Higher relative impact (as percentage of GDP) in developing economies
- Different recovery capacities across regions
- Varying levels of insurance protection

## Insurance Market Implications

### Current Insurance Landscape

- High premiums in high-risk areas
- Coverage exclusions and limitations
- Reinsurance capacity constraints

### Market Challenges

- Pricing difficulties due to changing risk profiles

### Innovation Opportunities
