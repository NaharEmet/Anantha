# Swiss Re Institute Report on Flood Risks (2021)

## Key Finding: Insurance Gap
- **75% of flood risks remain uninsured** globally
- This represents a substantial protection gap that leaves populations and economies vulnerable to financial impacts

## Context of 2021 Flood Events
- Extreme flood events drove significant economic losses in 2021
- These events contributed to the overall trend of increasing catastrophe losses
- Floods are becoming more frequent and severe due to climate change and other factors

## Global Risk Assessment
The Swiss Re Institute provides comprehensive assessment of global flood risks, including:
- Geographic distribution of flood exposure
- Economic vulnerability to flood events
- Insurance penetration rates across different regions
- Long-term trends in flood risk and economic impact

## Insurance Market Implications
The report highlights challenges and opportunities in the insurance market:
- The need for innovative insurance products to address the protection gap
- The role of governments in supporting risk management strategies
- The importance of preventive measures to reduce flood vulnerability

## Methodology
Swiss Re Institute utilizes advanced modeling techniques and comprehensive data to assess flood risks, including:
- Historical loss data
- Climate change projections
- Exposure mapping
- Economic vulnerability analysis

## Climate Change Context
The report places flood risk in the context of broader climate change impacts, noting how climate change is intensifying flood events through:
- Rising sea levels
- Increased precipitation intensity
- Changing weather patterns

## Economic Impact Assessment
The report provides detailed analysis of the economic consequences of flooding, including:
- Direct damages to property and infrastructure
- Indirect business interruption costs
- Long-term reconstruction and recovery costs
- Macro-economic impacts at regional and national levels
