# Munich Re Natural Disaster Figures 2023

## Global Losses in 2023
- Total economic losses from natural disasters worldwide: approximately US$ 250 billion
- Insured losses: US$ 95 billion
- This represents a return to the five-year average level of total losses
- Insured losses were slightly below the five-year average (US$ 105 billion)

## Comparison with Previous Year
- 2022 total losses: US$ 250 billion
- 2022 insured losses: US$ 125 billion
- The slight decrease in insured losses in 2023 compared to 2022 reflects the absence of major catastrophe events in 2023 that had occurred in 2022

## Regional Distribution
The report provides detailed analysis of natural disaster impacts across different global regions, including North America, Europe, Asia, and other areas.

## Key Disaster Types
The Munich Re report typically categorizes losses by type of natural disaster, including:
- Storms (including tropical cyclones, thunderstorms)
- Floods
- Earthquakes
- Wildfires
- Other natural catastrophes

## Insurance Implications
The report highlights the ongoing challenges in insuring natural catastrophe risks, particularly in regions with high exposure but lower insurance penetration rates.

## Methodology
Munich Re's natural disaster figures are based on comprehensive analysis of insured and uninsured losses from natural catastrophes worldwide, using standardized methodologies for consistent reporting across years.
