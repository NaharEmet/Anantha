# Munich Re Natural Disaster Figures 2024

## Global Losses in 2024
- Total economic losses from natural disasters worldwide: US$ 320 billion
- Insured losses: US$ 140 billion
- Both total losses and insured losses were considerably higher than in previous years

## Climate Change Impact
The report explicitly states that "climate change showed its claws in 2024," with well over 90% of the total losses of US$ 320 billion caused by weather-related catastrophes.

## Regional Analysis
The report provides detailed analysis of natural disaster impacts across different regions, with particular focus on:
- Asia-Pacific and Africa regions experiencing significant losses
- Severe thunderstorms and flooding driving much of the loss activity

## Insurance Gap
The report highlights the ongoing insurance gap, with a significant portion of disaster losses remaining uninsured, particularly in developing countries.

## Disaster Type Distribution
The report typically includes analysis of different types of natural disasters and their contribution to total losses, with weather-related events dominating the loss statistics.

## Methodology
Munich Re's natural disaster figures are based on comprehensive analysis of insured and uninsured losses from natural catastrophes worldwide, using standardized methodologies for consistent reporting across years and regions.

## Trend Analysis
The report positions 2024 data within the context of long-term trends, showing how climate change is contributing to more frequent and severe weather-related events with significant economic consequences.
