# WMO Atlas of Mortality and Economic Losses from Weather, Climate and Water-related Hazards (1970-2021)

## Overview
The WMO Atlas presents a comprehensive analysis of mortality and economic losses from weather, climate and water-related hazards over a 51-year period (1970-2021). This authoritative document provides the most complete picture of how climate-related disasters have evolved over time and their global impact.

## Key Global Statistics (1970-2021)

### Disasters
- **Total weather, climate and water-related disasters**: Nearly 12,000 events
- **Average annual disasters**: Approximately 235 events per year
- **Increase in frequency**: Clear upward trend over the period

### Mortality Impact
- **Total deaths**: Over 2 million people
- **Average annual deaths**: Approximately 40,000 people per year
- **Regional variation**: Asia bears the brunt of mortality (91% of total deaths)
- **Improvement trends**: Death toll has decreased in recent decades due to improved early warning systems

### Economic Impact
- **Total economic losses**: US$ 4.3 trillion
- **Average annual losses**: Approximately US$ 84 billion per year
- **Economic losses as percentage of GDP**: Varies significantly by region and country
- **Acceleration in recent years**: Economic losses have shown dramatic increase in recent decades

## Temporal Trends Analysis

### Frequency Trends
- **1970s**: Approximately 100 major disasters per decade
- **1980s**: Approximately 200 major disasters per decade
- **1990s**: Approximately 300 major disasters per decade
- **2000s**: Approximately 700 major disasters per decade
- **2010s**: Approximately 3,000 major disasters per decade
- **1970-2021**: Nearly 12,000 total events

### Economic Loss Trends
- **1970s**: Relatively low economic losses (adjusted for inflation)
- **1980s**: Moderate economic losses beginning to rise
- **1990s**: Significant increase in economic losses
- **2000s**: Dramatic increase in economic losses
- **2010s**: Exponential growth in economic losses
- **2020s**: Continued growth with particularly severe events

## Regional Distribution Analysis

### Asia
- **Disasters**: Highest frequency of events
- **Deaths**: 91% of all disaster-related deaths (1.8 million+)
- **Economic losses**: Significant but lower than Americas as percentage of GDP
- **Key hazards**: Tropical cyclones, floods, monsoon-related events
- **Notable trend**: High mortality but improving due to early warning systems

### Americas
- **Disasters**: High frequency of events
- **Deaths**: Relatively low mortality compared to population
- **Economic losses**: 41% of global total economic losses
- **Key hazards**: Tropical cyclones, floods, wildfires, severe storms
- **Notable trend**: High economic losses due to concentration of assets

### Africa
- **Disasters**: Moderate to high frequency
- **Deaths**: Significant mortality impact
- **Economic losses**: High as percentage of GDP but lower absolute values
- **Key hazards**: Droughts, floods, extreme temperatures
- **Notable trend**: High vulnerability due to limited adaptive capacity

### Europe
- **Disasters**: Moderate frequency
- **Deaths**: Lower mortality compared to other regions
- **Economic losses**: Significant absolute values
- **Key hazards**: Floods, heatwaves, storms, wildfires
- **Notable trend**: Increasing economic losses from climate-related events

### Oceania
- **Disasters**: Variable frequency
- **Deaths**: Lower absolute numbers but high per capita in some island nations
- **Economic losses**: High as percentage of GDP for small island states
- **Key hazards**: Tropical cyclones, floods, sea level rise impacts
- **Notable trend**: Particularly vulnerable to sea level rise and coastal hazards

## Hazard Type Analysis

### Weather-related Disasters (74% of economic losses)
- **Floods**: Leading cause of economic losses globally
- **Storms**: Including tropical cyclones and extratropical storms
- **Droughts**: Significant economic impacts, particularly in agriculture
- **Extreme temperatures**: Increasing economic impact from heatwaves and cold events
- **Wildfires**: Rapidly growing economic impact

### Climate-related Disasters
- **Long-term climate change impacts**: Gradual but cumulative effects
- **Sea level rise**: Coastal flooding and erosion
- **Glacial lake outburst floods**: Increasing in frequency
- **Permafrost degradation**: Infrastructure impacts in northern regions

### Water-related Disasters
- **Riverine flooding**: Most common and economically damaging
- **Flash floods**: High impact but localized events
- **Storm surges**: Coastal flooding from tropical cyclones
- **Drought-related water scarcity**: Agricultural and economic impacts

## Economic Impact Assessment

### Direct Economic Damages
- **Property destruction and damage**: Largest component of losses
- **Infrastructure damage**: Roads, bridges, utilities, buildings
- **Agricultural losses**: Crop damage, livestock loss, fisheries impact
- **Industrial disruption**: Business interruption and supply chain effects

### Indirect Economic Effects
- **Business interruption**: Lost production and services
- **Supply chain disruptions**: Secondary effects beyond immediate impact zone
- **Government response costs**: Emergency services and recovery spending
- **Long-term reconstruction**: Multi-year recovery and rebuilding

### Macroeconomic Impacts
- **GDP reduction**: Short-term and long-term impacts on economic growth
- **Fiscal pressure**: Government spending on disaster response and recovery
- **Inflationary effects**: Supply disruptions and reconstruction costs
- **Trade impacts**: Export disruptions and import needs

### Distributional Impacts
- **Poverty impacts**: Disproportionate effect on vulnerable populations
- **Regional inequality**: Widening gap between developed and developing regions
- **Gender impacts**: Different vulnerability and recovery patterns
- **Social cohesion**: Community resilience and social capital effects

## Climate Change Context

### Observed Changes
- **Increasing frequency**: Clear trend of more frequent events
- **Increasing intensity**: Some event types showing greater intensity
- **Changing patterns**: Seasonal and geographic shifts in hazard distribution
- **Compound events**: Multiple hazards occurring simultaneously or in sequence

### Climate Attribution
- **Human influence**: Scientific consensus on anthropogenic climate change
- **Specific attribution**: Complex but growing body of evidence linking specific events
- **Future projections**: Continued increase in frequency and intensity projected
- **Non-climate factors**: Population growth and development patterns also contributing

## Data Quality and Methodology

### Data Sources
- **National meteorological and hydrological services**: Primary data providers
- **United Nations agencies**: Additional data and verification
- **Academic research**: Peer-reviewed studies and assessments
- **Insurance industry**: Economic loss data where available

### Methodology
- **Standardized definitions**: Consistent disaster classification criteria
- **Economic normalization**: Adjusted for inflation where possible
- **Geographic coverage**: Global with varying completeness by region
- **Time period**: 1970-2021 with regular updates

### Limitations
- **Historical underreporting**: Earlier periods had less complete data
- **Economic assessment challenges**: Valuing intangible and indirect effects
- **Regional variations**: Different reporting capacities and methodologies
- **Event classification**: Some events may not fit perfectly into categories

## Policy Implications

### Early Warning Systems
- **Success stories**: Reduction in mortality from improved warnings
- **Remaining gaps**: Limited coverage in many regions
- **Technical capacity**: Need for improved monitoring and prediction
- **Communication effectiveness**: Ensuring warnings reach vulnerable populations

### Risk Reduction
- **Infrastructure resilience**: Building codes and engineering standards
- **Ecosystem-based approaches**: Natural solutions for risk reduction
- **Land use planning**: Avoiding development in high-risk areas
- **Community preparedness**: Local capacity for response and recovery

### Financial Protection
- **Insurance mechanisms**: Expanding coverage for vulnerable populations
- **International cooperation**: Global risk pooling arrangements
- **Government budgets**: Fiscal space for disaster response
- **Innovation**: New financial instruments for climate risk

### Adaptation Planning
- **National strategies**: Integration of disaster risk into development planning
- **Sector-specific approaches**: Different priorities by economic sector
- **Vulnerable populations**: Targeted protection for most at-risk communities
- **Long-term planning**: Climate-resilient development pathways

## Future Projections

### Climate Change Scenarios
- **Continued warming**: Further increase in extreme weather events
- **Sea level rise**: Accelerating coastal impacts
- **Precipitation changes**: More intense rainfall events and droughts
- **Compound events**: More frequent and severe multi-hazard events

### Economic Projections
- **Growing losses**: Continued increase in absolute economic costs
- **Regional variation**: Different patterns of impact across regions
- **Sectoral impacts**: Agriculture, infrastructure, and most vulnerable sectors
- **Adaptation dividend**: Reduction in losses with effective risk management

### Risk Management Evolution
- **Early warning expansion**: Improved coverage and effectiveness
- **Financial innovation**: New instruments for risk transfer
- **Technology integration**: AI, big data, and remote sensing
- **Governance improvements**: Institutional capacity and coordination
