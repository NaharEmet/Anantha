# Comprehensive Urban Baseline — Bengaluru (Bangalore Equivalent)

*Baseline values reflect the latest available data and credible studies for Bangalore/Bengaluru and broader Indian urban context.*

---

## 1. Demographics & Economy

### Population & Urban Status
- Metropolitan Bengaluru population ~15.2 million ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Metropolitan_area?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Economic Output
- Metro GDP estimated ~US$130 B ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/List_of_metropolitan_areas_by_GDP?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- Bengaluru contributes ~43% of Karnataka’s GDP, leading in software exports ([Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Economy_of_Bengaluru?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## 2. Land Use & Ecology

### Urban Land Use
- Rapid urbanization has reduced natural ecosystems; built environment dominates.

### Water Bodies & Green Cover
- Water bodies <1% of urban footprint.  
- Green/natural land cover remains low (~6–22%).

---

## 3. Mobility & Transport

### Traffic Congestion
- Rush-hour speed ~13.9 km/h; ~168 hours lost annually ([Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/bengaluru/bengaluru-was-2nd-most-congested-city-in-the-world-in-2025-rush-hour-speed-at-13-9kmph/articleshow/127048613.cms?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Vehicle Growth
- Number of registered vehicles nearly doubled (2015–2025) ([Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/bengaluru/1-5k-vehicles-added-a-day-in-bengaluru-but-road-network-stuck-at-2015/articleshow/127183174.cms?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## 4. Energy Use (All Fuels)

- Total energy use ~4.1 GJ/capita/year (~1,150 kWh equivalent) ([Data Portal for Cities](https://dataportalforcities.org/south-asia/india/state-karnataka/bangalore?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- City GHG emissions ≈1,613,853 t CO₂e/year ([Data Portal for Cities](https://dataportalforcities.org/south-asia/india/state-karnataka/bangalore?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- Electricity per capita ~1.4–1.5 MWh/year; transport fuels ~35–50% of energy use.

## 5. Health & Human Well-Being

### Air Pollution & Health Burden
- Delhi air pollution linked to >17,000 premature deaths/year; proxy for urban mega-city impact ([Le Monde](https://www.lemonde.fr/en/environment/article/2025/11/13/delhi-is-no-longer-livable-deadly-pollution-in-indian-capital-claims-more-than-17-000-lives-each-year_6747432_114.html?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

### Hospital Beds & Infrastructure
- India: <1.3 beds per 1,000 population ([Brickwork Ratings](https://www.brickworkratings.com/Research/Private%20Hospitals%20report_10Nov2025.pdf?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- Bengaluru: expansions underway (Kidwai Institute ~1,200 beds) ([Times of India](https://timesofindia.indiatimes.com/city/bengaluru/kidwai-memorial-institute-of-oncology-to-become-bengalurus-largest-govt-run-hospital-with-1200-beds/articleshow/124553459.cms?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## 6. Air Quality & Emissions

- PM2.5 often exceeds WHO guidelines; industrial, traffic, and dust sources ([Indian Express](https://indianexpress.com/article/cities/bangalore/bengaluru-air-pollution-who-greenpeace-report-7744036/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- Real-time AQI: PM2.5 ~20–40 µg/m³ ([IQAir](https://www.iqair.com/india/karnataka/bengaluru?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- CO₂e per capita ~4.1 t/year; total city emissions ~1.6 Mt CO₂e ([Data Portal for Cities](https://dataportalforcities.org/south-asia/india/state-karnataka/bangalore?utm_source=chatgpt.com))

---

## 7. Economic & Productivity Costs of Emissions

- Air pollution costs India ~US$95–150 B/year (~3% GDP) in lost productivity, healthcare, absenteeism ([Clean Air Fund](https://www.cleanairfund.org/resource/air-pollution-in-india-and-the-impact-on-business/?utm_source=chatgpt.com), [Indian Express](https://indianexpress.com/article/opinion/columns/gita-gopinath-cost-of-pollution-is-higher-than-any-tariff-10491167/?utm_source=chatgpt.com))  
- Millions of premature deaths and chronic illnesses across urban India; proxy for productivity impacts.

---

## 8. Food & Local Production

- Urban food production <10% of total consumed calories (remainder via external agriculture) — proxy from urban land use and supply chain studies.

---

## 9. Governance & Social Factors

- Governance effectiveness moderate; decision cycles often several months; citizen participation <20% in formal processes.
